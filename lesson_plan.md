## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

1. **Identify fact families** for addition/subtraction and multiplication/division
2. **Explain inverse relationships** ‚Äì how we can "undo" a calculation
3. **Convert repeated addition** (e.g., 5 + 5 + 5) into multiplication (3 √ó 5)
4. **Connect doubling** to the 2-times table

## Warm-Up: Fact Family Fast-Fire (8 minutes)

> **Teacher Script:** "Good morning, mathematicians! Today we're going to discover something magical about numbers ‚Äì they have FAMILIES! Let's see how fast you can find all the family members!"

### Activity Instructions

1. Display three numbers on the board: **15, 10, 5**
2. Challenge students to write **all 4 related equations** on mini whiteboards
3. Give 60 seconds, then reveal answers together

**Expected Answers:**
- 10 + 5 = 15
- 5 + 10 = 15
- 15 ‚àí 5 = 10
- 15 ‚àí 10 = 5

### Transition to Multiplication/Division

> **Teacher Script:** "Fantastic work! Now here's something exciting ‚Äì multiplication and division have fact families too!"

Display new numbers: **2, 5, 10**

**Expected Answers:**
- 2 √ó 5 = 10
- 5 √ó 2 = 10
- 10 √∑ 2 = 5
- 10 √∑ 5 = 2

> **Teacher Script:** "See how the same three numbers create four equations? This is the power of INVERSE operations!"

## Direct Instruction (15 minutes)

### Part A: The Inverse Equation Concept (5 mins)

> **Teacher Script:** "Inverse means OPPOSITE. Just like walking forward and then walking backward gets you back to where you started, we can UNDO calculations!"

**Demonstrate on board:**

| Action | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Start with 8 | 8 |
| Add 7 | 8 + 7 = **15** |
| Subtract 7 (UNDO) | 15 ‚àí 7 = **8** ‚úì Back to start! |

> **Key Point:** "Subtraction undoes addition. Division undoes multiplication."

### Part B: Repeated Addition ‚Üí Multiplication (5 mins)

> **Teacher Script:** "Now let's discover why multiplication is like a SHORTCUT!"

**Write on board:**
```
5 + 5 + 5 = 15

How many fives? THREE!
So we can write: 3 √ó 5 = 15
```

**Practice together:**
- 2 + 2 + 2 + 2 = ? ‚Üí **4 √ó 2 = 8**
- 10 + 10 + 10 = ? ‚Üí **3 √ó 10 = 30**

> **Teacher Script:** "Instead of adding the same number over and over, multiplication lets us do it in ONE step!"

### Part C: Doubling = The 2√ó Table (5 mins)

> **Teacher Script:** "When we DOUBLE a number, we're actually using the 2-times table!"

**Interactive demonstration:**

| Number | Double | 2√ó Table |
|--------|--------|----------|
| 3 | 3 + 3 = 6 | 2 √ó 3 = 6 ‚úì |
| 5 | 5 + 5 = 10 | 2 √ó 5 = 10 ‚úì |
| 7 | 7 + 7 = 14 | 2 √ó 7 = 14 ‚úì |

> **Key Point:** "Double means TWO of something ‚Äì that's 2 times!"

## Teacher Notes

### Key Vocabulary
- **Fact Family** ‚Äì A set of related equations using the same numbers
- **Inverse** ‚Äì The opposite operation that "undoes" a calculation
- **Array** ‚Äì Objects arranged in rows and columns
- **Repeated Addition** ‚Äì Adding the same number multiple times
- **Double** ‚Äì Two of something; multiply by 2

### Common Misconceptions to Address
1. **"3 √ó 4 means 3 added 4 times"** ‚Äì Clarify: It means 3 groups OF 4, or 4 repeated 3 times
2. **Confusing rows and columns in arrays** ‚Äì Use consistent language: "rows OF"
3. **Not recognizing inverse relationships** ‚Äì Use physical "undo" demonstrations

### Cross-Curricular Links
- **Literacy:** Vocabulary building with math terms
- **Art:** Creating array patterns and designs
- **Music:** Counting beats in groups (rhythm patterns)
